What is Climate Change?

A change in global or regional climate patterns, in particular a change apparent from the mid to late 20th century onwards and attributed largely to the increased levels of atmospheric carbon dioxide produced by the use of fossil fuels.

Causes of Climate Change

There are many causes of climate change:

Deforestation.

The large-scale removal of forests, known as deforestation, is a major contributor to climate change. Forests act as carbon sinks, absorbing carbon dioxide, and their destruction releases stored carbon back into the atmosphere.

Greenhouse gas.

The release of greenhouse gases, such as carbon dioxide and methane, into the Earth's atmosphere contributes significantly to climate change. These gases trap heat, leading to a warming effect known as the greenhouse effect.

Industrial.

Various industrial processes, including manufacturing and energy production, release substantial amounts of pollutants and greenhouse gases. These emissions contribute to the overall environmental changes associated with climate change.

  • Effects of Climate Change

    Disruption of ecosystems:
    Climate change disrupts ecosystems by altering temperature and precipitation patterns. This can lead to shifts in plant and animal habitats, affecting their distribution and abundance.

  • Effects of Climate Change

    Threats to Biodiversity: Climate change poses a significant threat to biodiversity by changing the conditions under which species have evolved. Altered temperatures, habitat loss, and other environmental changes can lead to the decline or extinction of various plant and animal species, disrupting the balance of ecosystems.

  • Effects of Climate Change

    Rising sea levels:
    Rising sea levels result from the Earth's warming, causing the melting of ice caps and glaciers. This can lead to flooding, affecting coastal areas and river deltas, impacting the lives of communities and infrastructure.
